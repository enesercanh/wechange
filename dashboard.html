  • University of Baghdad hosts a new research conference.

    Details:

    By Haneen Abdulqader

    The University of Baghdad successfully hosted its inaugural Research Conference last week, bringing together experts, researchers, and students from across the nation to discuss and showcase innovative research. The event, held in the university's Main Conference Hall, was a tremendous success, providing a platform for in-depth discussions on various scientific, technological, and social issues.

    The conference kicked off with a keynote speech by Dr. Ahmed Al-Saadi, a leading researcher in the field of renewable energy, who emphasized the importance of collaborative research in solving the world's most pressing challenges. "Research is the key to unlocking the solutions to today's problems. It’s through sharing knowledge and working together that we can make lasting change," Dr. Al-Saadi remarked in his opening address.

    Over the course of the day, attendees were treated to a series of presentations and workshops that spanned a wide range of topics, including environmental sustainability, artificial intelligence, healthcare innovations, and social justice. The presentations showcased some of the latest research from Iraqi scholars, as well as contributions from international researchers who have collaborated with local institutions.

    One of the highlights of the conference was the session on "Technology for Social Good," where researchers presented new tools and applications aimed at addressing social challenges in Iraq. The session sparked lively discussions among students, academics, and professionals, all eager to explore how technology can be harnessed to create a more inclusive and sustainable society.

    In addition to the academic presentations, the conference also featured several panel discussions, providing a platform for thought leaders to engage in debates on the future of research in Iraq and the Middle East. These discussions covered topics such as funding for research, the role of academia in national development, and strategies for overcoming obstacles in research methodologies.

    The conference concluded with a closing ceremony, where the university's rector, Dr. Yaseen Al-Jabari, praised the event’s success and expressed the institution's commitment to fostering a culture of research and innovation. "This conference is only the beginning. We will continue to support research efforts that contribute to the development of Iraq and the global community," Dr. Al-Jabari stated.

    The event was widely regarded as a step forward for the academic community in Iraq, strengthening connections between universities, researchers, and industry professionals. As the conference came to a close, many attendees expressed their excitement for the future of research in Iraq, eager to collaborate and build upon the ideas shared during the event.

  • American University of Iraq Baghdad launches a new career fair program.

    Details:

    By Sami Taha

    The American University of Iraq, Baghdad (AUIB) has launched an exciting new career fair program aimed at connecting students with leading employers and offering valuable career development opportunities. The event, held in the university's main atrium, welcomed over 30 companies from a variety of sectors, including technology, finance, healthcare, and engineering.

    The inaugural career fair, which took place last Friday, drew hundreds of students eager to explore potential job and internship opportunities. The program was designed to not only provide students with direct access to employers but also to offer workshops and seminars on career planning, resume building, and interview techniques.

    In his opening remarks, Dr. Nabil Shakir, President of AUIB, emphasized the importance of bridging the gap between academic learning and professional success. "Our goal is to ensure that every student has the tools and resources they need to transition from the classroom to the workforce with confidence," Dr. Shakir stated.

    The career fair featured booths set up by top companies looking to recruit fresh talent from the university’s diverse pool of students. Among the prominent employers present were major corporations like Zain Iraq, Asiacell, and several international non-governmental organizations (NGOs). Students were able to engage in one-on-one conversations with recruiters, ask about job openings, and learn about the skills and qualifications needed to excel in various industries.

    In addition to the recruitment booths, the career fair also featured a series of interactive workshops that focused on professional development. These sessions included advice on how to effectively network, tips on improving LinkedIn profiles, and strategies for acing interviews. The workshops were well-received, with many students expressing their appreciation for the practical guidance offered by the speakers.

    The highlight of the day was the panel discussion titled "The Future of Work in Iraq," which brought together industry leaders and human resources professionals to discuss the evolving job market in the country. Panelists discussed the growing demand for skilled workers in fields such as cybersecurity, digital marketing, and renewable energy, and shared insights on how graduates can stay competitive in an increasingly globalized economy.

    One of the attendees, Layla Ahmed, a senior student in the business administration program, shared her thoughts on the event: "This career fair has been incredibly valuable for me. It’s not just about finding a job, but about learning how to present myself professionally and prepare for the workforce. I feel much more confident about my future now."

    The success of the first-ever AUIB Career Fair has set the stage for future events, with the university planning to make this program an annual occurrence. As the event concluded, Dr. Shakir reiterated the institution’s commitment to supporting students in their career journeys, adding, "This is just the beginning. We will continue to provide platforms for our students to grow and thrive in their professional lives."

  • University of Technology students win national awards in tech innovation.

    Details:

    By Anas Malik

    Students from the University of Technology in Baghdad have triumphed at the National Tech Innovation Awards, taking home several top honors for their cutting-edge projects in technology. The event, held at the National Tech Expo earlier this week, celebrated the best tech innovations from across Iraq, with the University of Technology standing out for their contributions to robotics, AI, and sustainable engineering.

    One of the most impressive projects was developed by a team of engineering students who created an intelligent waste management system designed to reduce urban waste through advanced sensor technologies and automated sorting. This innovative project caught the attention of the judges for its potential to address one of Iraq's growing environmental concerns. "Our goal was to find a technological solution that could directly impact the environment and contribute to building a sustainable future," said Sara Jassim, the project leader.

    In addition, a group of computer science students received an award for developing an AI-powered app aimed at improving healthcare accessibility. The app, which uses machine learning algorithms, helps diagnose common medical conditions based on user symptoms, offering affordable healthcare assistance in remote and underserved areas. "We wanted to make healthcare more accessible and affordable for everyone, especially in rural communities," said Hassan Shakir, one of the developers.

    The students' success at the national awards has already drawn praise from industry experts. Dr. Nadia Al-Mansoori, a well-known tech entrepreneur and one of the event’s judges, expressed her admiration for the students’ dedication and creativity. "The innovation showcased by these students is truly remarkable. Their work demonstrates not only technical skill but a deep commitment to solving real-world problems," she commented.

    University of Technology’s President, Dr. Omar Al-Hassan, also celebrated the achievements, highlighting the institution's growing reputation for fostering talent in tech and innovation. "These awards are a testament to the hard work and dedication of our students and faculty. We are proud to contribute to Iraq's growing tech ecosystem and will continue to support our students in their future endeavors," Dr. Al-Hassan said during the closing ceremony.

    The victory of these students marks a significant milestone for the University of Technology, reinforcing the importance of supporting and nurturing young innovators in Iraq. With these groundbreaking projects, the university's students are not only helping to shape the future of technology but are also making meaningful contributions to the development of Iraq’s economy and society.
